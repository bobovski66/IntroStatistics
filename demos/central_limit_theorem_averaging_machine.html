The Central Limit Theorem

When we repeatedly take random samples of size n and compute the sample mean, those sample means tend toward an approximately normal distribution as n grows. This holds even when the original population is not normal.

Individual observations may be noisy; averages become more stable and predictable.

σx̄ = σ / √n

The standard deviation of the sample mean is the standard error. Try n = 1, then n = 5, then n = 30, and watch how the histogram of x̄ narrows and normalizes.
