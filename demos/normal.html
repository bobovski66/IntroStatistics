Normal Distribution Explorer

Adjust the mean and standard deviation to see how a normal model moves, spreads out, and assigns probability to intervals. The shaded region represents the probability between the lower and upper cutoffs.

f(x) = 1/(σ√(2π)) · e−(x−μ)²/(2σ²)

How to read the picture

The curve is density, not probability. Probability comes from area under the curve.

μ shifts the center. σ controls spread. Larger σ means a wider, flatter curve.

Center

Changing μ moves the model

The mean is the balance point of the curve. Moving μ left or right changes the typical value but does not change the overall shape.

Spread

Changing σ changes variability

A smaller σ concentrates observations near the mean. A larger σ spreads them out and lowers the peak.

Area

Probability is area

The total area under the curve is 1. The shaded area estimates the chance that a randomly chosen value falls in the selected interval.

Teaching tip: after students use the ±1σ, ±2σ, and ±3σ buttons, ask them to connect the observed percentages to the empirical rule: about 68%, 95%, and 99.7%.
