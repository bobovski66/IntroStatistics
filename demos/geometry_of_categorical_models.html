Geometry of Categorical Models

A categorical model is a point in a shape. With 2 categories the shape is a line segment, with 3 categories it is a triangle, and with 4 categories it is a tetrahedron. This demo shows the expected model point, the observed data point, and the chi-squared distance between them.

1. Choose the model size

The total sample size is fixed at 60. That is why one count is always dependent on the others.

2. What the picture means

Chi-squared: χ2 = Σ (O - E)2 / E
Degrees of freedom: df = k - 1

The expected counts define a target point in the shape. The observed counts define another point. The chi-squared statistic measures a weighted distance from observed to expected.
