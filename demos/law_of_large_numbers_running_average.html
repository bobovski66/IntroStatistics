The Law of Large Numbers

As we repeat the same random experiment more and more times, the average of the outcomes tends to get closer to the true expected value. Short runs can be wild; the point is that the long-run average becomes stable.

average ≈ expected value

Written in symbols, the sample average x̄ converges to the expected value μ as the number of trials grows. Try stopping the demo after 10, 100, and 1000 trials and compare the distance from μ.
