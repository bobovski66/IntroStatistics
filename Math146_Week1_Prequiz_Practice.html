Problem 1 Population, sample, and notation
A county health department wants to estimate the average number of hours of sleep adults in the county get per night. They recruit 120 randomly selected adults and record how many hours each person slept last night.
(a) Identify the population in this study.
Show solution

All adults in the county.

The population is the entire group we want to draw conclusions about — every adult in the county, not just those in the study.

(b) Identify the sample.
Show solution

The 120 randomly selected adults who participated in the study.

The sample is the subset of the population that we actually observe. The sample gives us information about the population, but it is not the population.

(c) The department finds the average sleep time for the 120 participants is 6.8 hours. Which symbol represents this value — μ or x? Explain why.
Show solution

x = 6.8 hours.

This is a sample statistic — a number computed from sample data. We use x (read: "x-bar") for statistics calculated from a sample.

μ is reserved for the true population mean, which we typically do not know and are trying to estimate.

(d) The true average sleep time for all adults in the county is unknown. Which Greek letter would we use to represent this unknown value?
Show solution

μ (mu).

Greek letters denote population parameters — quantities describing the entire population. Since we cannot measure every adult in the county, μ remains unknown; we use x from our sample to estimate it.

Problem 2 Summation notation and frequency
Ten students report the number of cups of coffee they drank yesterday:
0, 1, 2, 1, 3, 0, 2, 1, 0, 2
(a) Compute Σi = 110 xi.
Show solution

Σ xi = 0 + 1 + 2 + 1 + 3 + 0 + 2 + 1 + 0 + 2

= 12

(b) Compute Σi = 110 xi2.
Show solution

Square each value first, then sum:

02 + 12 + 22 + 12 + 32 + 02 + 22 + 12 + 02 + 22

= 0 + 1 + 4 + 1 + 9 + 0 + 4 + 1 + 0 + 4

= 24

Note: Σ xi2 ≠ (Σ xi)2. Here 122 = 144, which is very different from 24.

(d) What percentage of students drank at least one cup of coffee?
Show solution

"At least one cup" means 1, 2, or 3 cups. From the table:

Frequency = 3 + 3 + 1 = 7 students out of 10.

Relative frequency = 7/10 = 0.70, so 70% of students drank at least one cup.

Equivalently: only 3 students drank 0 cups, so 10 − 3 = 7 drank at least one.

Problem 3 Mean and standard deviation
Five friends compare the number of text messages they sent yesterday:
12, 25, 8, 31, 14
(a) Compute the sample mean x.
Show solution

x = Σ xin = 12 + 25 + 8 + 31 + 145 = 905

x = 18 text messages.

The sum of deviations is always 0 — a useful check. We divide by n − 1 = 4 (not n) because this is a sample standard deviation.

(c) A sixth friend joins and says they sent 500 text messages. Without computing, does adding this person increase, decrease, or not change x? Do you expect s to increase or decrease? Briefly explain.
Show solution

x will increase; s will increase dramatically.

The current mean is 18. Adding 500 — far above the mean — pulls the mean upward. It cannot stay the same or decrease.

For s: standard deviation measures spread around the mean. The value 500 is an extreme outlier, so its squared deviation is enormous, and s grows substantially.

This illustrates that both the mean and standard deviation are sensitive to outliers — a single extreme value can shift them considerably.
