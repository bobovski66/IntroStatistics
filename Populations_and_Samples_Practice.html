Populations, Samples, and Descriptive Statistics

Try each problem before opening the solution. Focus on the story: what is the population, what is the sample, and what do the numbers actually say in context?

Problem 1: Campus Coffee Quality

The student government wants to know how many students think the coffee on campus is “good or excellent.” There are about 20,000 students enrolled at the college.

They send a short online survey to a random sample of 1,000 students. Out of those, 640 say the campus coffee is “good or excellent.”

  1. Identify the population and the sample in this situation.
  2. Define the population parameter of interest using a symbol and a clear sentence.
  3. Compute the sample statistic that estimates this parameter (use standard notation and give the numerical value).
  4. Write one sentence interpreting your sample statistic in context (no symbols).
Show solution

(a) Population and sample

  • Population: all 20,000 students enrolled at the college.
  • Sample: the 1,000 students who responded to the survey.

(b) Population parameter

We care about the true proportion of all students who think the coffee is “good or excellent.” Let \[ p = \text{proportion of all students who rate the coffee as good or excellent}. \] This $p$ is a population parameter (unknown).

(c) Sample statistic

In the sample, 640 out of 1,000 students gave that response. The sample proportion is \[ \hat p = \frac{640}{1000} = 0.64. \] Here $\hat p$ is the statistic we use to estimate $p$.

(d) Interpretation in words

In our sample, 64% of the surveyed students said the campus coffee is good or excellent.

Later in the course, we will ask how close we think $\hat p = 0.64$ is to the true population proportion $p$.

Problem 2: Weekly Reading Challenge

A reading group on campus challenges students to read more for fun. At the end of a week, the facilitator asks 6 randomly chosen participants how many hours they spent reading non-school books that week.

Their answers (in hours) are:

Student 123456
Hours $x_i$ 456789
  1. Compute the sample mean number of hours $\bar x$.
  2. Compute the deviations $x_i - \bar x$ and use them to compute the sample variance $s^2$ and standard deviation $s$.
  3. Interpret $\bar x$ and $s$ in the context of this reading challenge.
Show solution

(a) Sample mean

The hours are $4,5,6,7,8,9$. The sample mean is \[ \bar x = \frac{4 + 5 + 6 + 7 + 8 + 9}{6} = \frac{39}{6} = 6.5. \] So on average, these students read 6.5 hours that week.

(b) Sample variance and standard deviation

First compute the deviations:

  • $4 - 6.5 = -2.5$
  • $5 - 6.5 = -1.5$
  • $6 - 6.5 = -0.5$
  • $7 - 6.5 = 0.5$
  • $8 - 6.5 = 1.5$
  • $9 - 6.5 = 2.5$

Square them and add:

\[ (-2.5)^2 + (-1.5)^2 + (-0.5)^2 + (0.5)^2 + (1.5)^2 + (2.5)^2 = 6.25 + 2.25 + 0.25 + 0.25 + 2.25 + 6.25 = 17.5. \]

The sample variance is \[ s^2 = \frac{17.5}{n-1} = \frac{17.5}{5} = 3.5. \] So the sample standard deviation is \[ s = \sqrt{3.5} \approx 1.87. \]

(c) Interpretation

The average amount of reading for these 6 students was 6.5 hours that week. The standard deviation of about 1.9 hours means that a typical student's reading time is about 2 hours away from that average.

In other words, many students are between about $6.5 - 1.9 \approx 4.6$ and $6.5 + 1.9 \approx 8.4$ hours of reading.

Problem 3: Parameter or Statistic?

For each statement below, decide whether the number described is a parameter or a statistic. Briefly explain your reasoning.

  1. “Every single seat in the college theater (all 150 seats) was checked, and 60% of the seats were found to have at least one broken armrest.”
  2. “A random sample of 80 students from the college found that their average commuting time was 39.2 minutes.”
  3. “Exactly 32% of all currently enrolled students at the college are taking at least one online class this term.”
Show solution

(1) Theater seat inspection

The statement says that every single seat in the theater (all 150) was checked, and 60% had a broken armrest. This describes the entire population of seats, not just a sample.

$\Rightarrow$ This is a parameter (a population-level number).

(2) Average commute from a random sample

Here we only have a random sample of 80 students from the larger population of all students. The 39.2 minutes is computed from sample data and is meant to estimate the population mean commuting time.

$\Rightarrow$ This is a statistic (a number from a sample).

(3) 32% of all students taking an online class

The phrasing “exactly 32% of all currently enrolled students” describes the entire population of students. In reality, we might not know this number perfectly, but if it were known, it would be population-level.

$\Rightarrow$ This is a parameter.

Problem 4: Time in the Campus Fitness Center

The director of the fitness center wants a snapshot of how much time students actually spend in the gym on a typical weekday.

On a randomly selected Tuesday, staff record how many minutes each of 10 randomly chosen students spend inside the fitness center that day:

Student 12345 678910
Minutes $x_i$ 3045606075 90120120150180
  1. Compute the sample mean number of minutes $\bar x$ that these students spend in the gym.
  2. Using the fact that $\sum_{i=1}^{10} (x_i - \bar x)^2 = 21060$, compute the sample variance $s^2$ and standard deviation $s$.
  3. Interpret $\bar x$ and $s$ in the context of time spent in the fitness center.
Show solution

(a) Sample mean

The total number of minutes is \[ 30 + 45 + 60 + 60 + 75 + 90 + 120 + 120 + 150 + 180 = 930. \] With $n = 10$ students, \[ \bar x = \frac{930}{10} = 93. \] So on average, these students spent 93 minutes in the fitness center that day.

(b) Sample variance and standard deviation

We are told that \[ \sum_{i=1}^{10} (x_i - \bar x)^2 = 21060. \] The sample variance is \[ s^2 = \frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{i=1}^{10} (x_i - \bar x)^2 = \frac{21060}{10 - 1} = \frac{21060}{9} = 2340. \] So the standard deviation is \[ s = \sqrt{2340} \approx 48.37. \] We might round this to $s \approx 48.4$ minutes.

(c) Interpretation

The mean $\bar x = 93$ minutes means that, on that Tuesday, the typical student in this sample spent about an hour and a half in the fitness center.

The standard deviation $s \approx 48.4$ minutes tells us that there is a lot of variation: a typical student's time is about 48 minutes away from the mean. Some students stayed much less than 93 minutes, and some stayed much longer.
