What you are practicing

In this activity, you will use two continuous probability distributions:

  1. For a uniform distribution, probability is the length of the desired interval divided by the total length.
  2. For a normal distribution, probability is area under the bell curve.
Continuous probability rule: For a continuous random variable, \(P(X=a)=0\). Probabilities come from intervals and are represented by areas.

Part 1. Uniform distribution

Let \(T\) be a time of day measured in decimal hours. Suppose every time from midnight to midnight is equally likely:

\[T\sim\operatorname{Uniform}(0,24)\]

The total width is 24 hours, so the rectangle must have height

\[\text{height}=\frac{1}{24}\approx 0.0417\]

Worked example

Find the probability that a report occurs between 8:00 a.m. and 2:00 p.m.

\[P(8\le T\le14)=\frac{14-8}{24}=\frac{6}{24}=0.25\]

So the model gives a 25% probability.
