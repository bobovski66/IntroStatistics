Part 2. Probabilities, expected value, and variance

\[E(X)=\sum_x x\,p(x),\qquad E(X^2)=\sum_x x^2p(x),\qquad \operatorname{Var}(X)=E(X^2)-[E(X)]^2\]

Part 3. Binomial and geometric models

Call a selection a success when observed_from = Air. The table contains 16 air-based entries among 121 total entries.

Binomial model

Imagine selecting 10 entries independently with replacement. Let \(Y\) be the number selected that were observed from the air. Model \(Y\) with \(Y\sim\operatorname{Binomial}(n=10,p=0.1322)\).

\[P(Y=k)=\binom{10}{k}p^k(1-p)^{10-k}\]

Geometric model

Let \(G\) be the number of selections required to obtain the first air-based report.

\[P(G=k)=(1-p)^{k-1}p,\qquad P(G>k)=(1-p)^k\]

The model answers unlock after the numerical answers are correct.

Binomial: \(P(Y=0)=0.2421,\ P(Y=1)=0.3690,\ P(Y\ge2)=0.3889,\ \mu=1.322,\ \sigma=1.071.\)
Geometric: \(P(G=1)=0.1322,\ P(G=5)=0.0750,\ P(G>5)=0.4921,\ E(G)=7.562\) selections.
