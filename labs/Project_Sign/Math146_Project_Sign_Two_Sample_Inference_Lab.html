Before you begin

This activity divides the 102 dated entries with exact object counts into two separate periods:

  • Group 1: January 16 through July 4, 1947
  • Group 2: July 5, 1947 through January 11, 1948

The cutoff gives both groups at least 30 entries and also provides enough single-object and multiple-object reports for the two-proportion procedure.

Interpret carefully: These are archival report entries, not random samples from all aerial events. Your conclusions apply to the stated comparison model.

Objectives

  • Distinguish independent samples from paired samples.
  • Conduct a two-sample \(z\)-test comparing two means.
  • Construct a confidence interval for \(\mu_1-\mu_2\).
  • Conduct a two-sample \(z\)-test comparing two proportions.
  • Explain how the numerical and categorical comparisons describe the same change differently.

Reference formulas

\[ \operatorname{SE}_{\bar x_1-\bar x_2} =\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1}+\frac{\sigma_2^2}{n_2}}, \qquad z=\frac{(\bar x_1-\bar x_2)-0}{\operatorname{SE}} \] \[ (\bar x_1-\bar x_2)\pm z^*\operatorname{SE} \] \[ \hat p_{\mathrm{pool}}=\frac{x_1+x_2}{n_1+n_2}, \qquad z=\frac{\hat p_1-\hat p_2} {\sqrt{\hat p_{\mathrm{pool}}(1-\hat p_{\mathrm{pool}}) \left(\frac1{n_1}+\frac1{n_2}\right)}} \]

The before-and-after quiz data are paired. The two class sections and the two Project Sign periods are independent because no observation in one group is naturally matched with a specific observation in the other.

Part 2. Two-sample test for mean object count

Test whether the earlier period has a larger mean number of objects reported.

\[ H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2 \qquad\text{versus}\qquad H_a:\mu_1>\mu_2 \]

Use \(\alpha=0.05\). For this activity, use the supplied group standard deviations as \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_2\).

Group\(n\)MeanSupplied \(\sigma\)
1: through July 44.643

\[ \bar x_1-\bar x_2=2.697, \] \[ \operatorname{SE} =\sqrt{\frac{4.643^2}{31}+\frac{1.481^2}{71}} =0.852, \] \[ z=\frac{2.697}{0.852}=3.164, \qquad p=P(Z\ge 3.164)=0.0008. \] Since \(p<0.05\), reject \(H_0\).
