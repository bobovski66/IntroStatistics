Problem 1 — Independent or Paired?

Before choosing a two-sample method, decide whether the two sets of data are independent or paired. Independent samples come from separate groups. Paired data come from matched measurements, such as before-and-after measurements on the same people.

(a) The same 30 students take a short quiz before and after a review session.
(b) Two separate class sections use different textbooks and their quiz scores are compared.
(c) Each patient has blood pressure measured before and after taking a medication.
(d) Ballot measure support surveyed in two different cities.
