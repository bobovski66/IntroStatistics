Objectives

  • Construct a confidence interval for a population mean and state its margin of error.
  • Explain how the confidence level affects the width of an interval.
  • Determine the sample size needed to achieve a target margin of error for a mean.
  • Construct and interpret a confidence interval for a population proportion.

Reference Formulas

CI for a mean (\(\sigma\) known): \[ \bar{x}\pm z^*\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \]

CI for a proportion: \[ \hat{p}\pm z^*\sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} \]

Required sample size: \[ n\ge\left(\frac{z^*\sigma}{\text{ME}}\right)^2 \]

Critical values: \(90\%: z^*=1.645\)   \(95\%: z^*=1.96\)   \(99\%: z^*=2.576\)

Problem 1 — Confidence Interval for a Population Mean

A school counselor samples 36 students and finds a mean sleep time of \(\bar{x}=6.8\) hours. Assume the population standard deviation is known to be \(\sigma=1.5\) hours. Use this sample to construct a 95% confidence interval for the true mean sleep time of students at the school.
(a) Construct a 95% confidence interval for \(\mu\).
Show solution

95% CI: \((6.31,\,7.29)\) hours; ME \(=0.49\) hours.

\(\text{SE}=1.5/\sqrt{36}=1.5/6=0.25\). \(\text{ME}=1.96\times0.25=0.49\). The interval is \(\bar{x}\pm\text{ME}=6.8\pm0.49\).

(b) State the margin of error.
Show solution

ME \(=0.49\) hours.

\(\text{ME}=z^*\times\text{SE}=1.96\times0.25=0.49\).

(c) Interpret your interval in context. Write one complete sentence including the confidence level, the population, and both bounds.
Show solution

We are 95% confident that the true mean sleep time of students at the school is between 6.31 and 7.29 hours.

A correct interpretation names the confidence level, the population parameter being estimated (the true mean, not an individual student's sleep), and both interval bounds.

Problem 2 — Confidence Level and Width

A sample of 64 packages has mean weight \(\bar{x}=12.4\) ounces. Assume the population standard deviation is \(\sigma=1.2\) ounces. You will use the same data to compare a 90% confidence interval with a 99% confidence interval and explain how confidence level affects interval width.
shared Find the standard error used by both intervals.
(a) Construct the 90% confidence interval for \(\mu\).
Show solution

90% CI: \((12.153,\,12.647)\).

\(\text{SE}=1.2/8=0.15\). \(\text{ME}=1.645\times0.15=0.247\), giving \(12.4\pm0.247\).

(b) Construct the 99% confidence interval for \(\mu\).
Show solution

99% CI: \((12.014,\,12.786)\). The 99% interval is wider.

\(\text{ME}=2.576\times0.15=0.386\), giving \(12.4\pm0.386\). Higher confidence requires a larger \(z^*\), which produces a larger margin of error and therefore a wider interval.

(c) Which interval is wider, and why? Name the wider interval and explain the trade-off between confidence level and width.
Show solution

The 99% interval is wider.

Higher confidence means we want to be more certain the interval captures the true mean, which forces a larger critical value \(z^*\) and therefore a larger margin of error — the price of higher confidence is a wider interval.

Problem 3 — Required Sample Size for a Mean

A transportation planner wants to estimate the mean commute time for workers in a city. The estimate should be within \(\text{ME}=2\) minutes at 95% confidence. Historical data suggest \(\sigma=10\) minutes. Find the minimum sample size needed to meet this margin of error.
(a) Find the required sample size.
Show solution

\(n=97\).

\(n\ge\left(\dfrac{1.96\times10}{2}\right)^2=(9.8)^2=96.04\). We always round up: \(n=96\) would give \(\text{ME}>2\), violating the requirement, so \(n=97\).

(b) Why do we round up rather than down?
Show solution

Rounding down would leave the margin of error too large.

The sample-size formula gives the smallest \(n\) that guarantees the margin of error is at most the target value. Since a larger \(n\) only shrinks the margin of error further, rounding up preserves the guarantee; rounding down would produce a margin of error that exceeds what was required.

Problem 4 — Confidence Interval for a Population Proportion

In a survey of 250 adults, 145 report that they check the weather forecast on their phone every day. Use this sample to estimate the population proportion of adults who check the weather forecast on their phone daily.
(a) Find the sample proportion \(\hat{p}\).
Show solution

\(\hat{p}=0.58\).

\(145/250=0.58\).

(b) Check the success–failure condition.
Show solution

\(n\hat{p}=145\ge10\) and \(n(1-\hat{p})=105\ge10\); the normal approximation is valid.

Both expected counts comfortably clear the threshold of 10, so we may proceed with a normal-based confidence interval for the proportion.

(c) Construct a 95% CI for \(p\).
Show solution

95% CI: \((0.519,\,0.641)\).

\(\hat{p}=0.58\); \(n\hat{p}=145\ge10\) and \(n(1-\hat{p})=105\ge10\). \(\text{SE}=\sqrt{0.58\times0.42/250}\approx0.0312\), and \(\text{ME}=1.96\times0.0312\approx0.0612\).

(d) Interpret your interval in context. Write one complete sentence naming the population, the variable, the confidence level, and both bounds.
Show solution

We are 95% confident that between 51.9% and 64.1% of adults check the weather forecast on their phone daily.

A complete interpretation names the population (adults), the variable (checking the forecast daily), the confidence level, and both bounds — without claiming certainty about any individual adult.
