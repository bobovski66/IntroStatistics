# Intro to Statistics

This repository contains interactive HTML labs, demonstrations, and textbook chapters for an introductory statistics course.

The materials are designed to support student learning through guided practice, worked examples, interactive activities, and textbook-style reference material. The organizing idea is to treat statistics as a way of reasoning from data: describing variation, modeling chance, estimating unknown quantities, and making evidence-based decisions.

## Interactive Demonstrations

These demonstrations are stored in the `demos/` directory. The order below follows the general topic sequence of the course: populations and samples, sample statistics, discrete probability models, long-run behavior, normal distributions, the Central Limit Theorem, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, and categorical models.

- [Galaxies Torus Demo](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/galaxies_torus.html)  
  A visual introduction to structured data and geometric organization.

- [Sample Statistics Tool](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/Sample_Statistics.html)  
  Compute and compare sample statistics from data.

- [Binomial vs. Geometric Distributions](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/binomial_vs_geometric.html)  
  Compare two common discrete probability models.

- [Law of Large Numbers Running Average](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/law_of_large_numbers_running_average.html)  
  Watch long-run averages settle as more trials are collected.

- [Galton Board](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/galton_board.html)  
  Explore how repeated random steps produce a bell-shaped distribution.

- [Normal Distribution Demo](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/normal.html)  
  Work with normal curves, areas, and z-scores.

- [Central Limit Theorem Averaging Machine](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/central_limit_theorem_averaging_machine.html)  
  See how sample means become more normal as sample size increases.

- [Confidence Intervals](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/ci_demo.html)  
  Explore confidence intervals and their interpretation.

- [Z-Test and Confidence Interval Demo](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/ztest_ci_demo.html)  
  Connect hypothesis tests, p-values, and confidence intervals.

- [Geometry of Categorical Models](https://bobovski66.github.io/IntroStatistics/demos/geometry_of_categorical_models.html)  
  Visualize categorical distributions, expected counts, and chi-squared distance.

## About the Course

The course emphasizes statistical reasoning, clear interpretation, and the use of real or realistic data. Students practice computing and interpreting sample statistics, working with probability models, using normal and related distributions, building confidence intervals, conducting hypothesis tests, and thinking carefully about variation in data.
