Standard Error Decision Flowchart

Use this guide to decide which standard error formula belongs with a mean, proportion, one-sample problem, or two-sample problem.

Standard error measures how much a sample statistic varies from sample to sample.
START

What kind of statistic are you working with?

One Sample Mean

Are you given the population standard deviation \( \sigma \)?

Yes: \( \sigma \) is known

Use the standard error

\[ SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \]

Use this for \(z\)-procedures:

\[ z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{SE} \]

No: \( \sigma \) is unknown

Use the sample standard deviation \(s\):

\[ SE = \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \]

Use this for \(t\)-procedures:

\[ t = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{SE} \]

Degrees of freedom: \(df = n - 1\).

One Sample Proportion

Are you working with a sample proportion \( \hat{p} \)?

Confidence interval

Use the sample proportion:

\[ SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} \]

Hypothesis test

Use the null proportion \(p_0\):

\[ SE = \sqrt{\frac{p_0(1-p_0)}{n}} \]

Test statistic:

\[ z = \frac{\hat{p} - p_0}{SE} \]
Two Sample Means

Are the population standard deviations \( \sigma_1 \) and \( \sigma_2 \) known?

Yes: \( \sigma_1 \) and \( \sigma_2 \) are known

Use the standard error

\[ SE = \sqrt{\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma_2^2}{n_2}} \]

Use this for \(z\)-procedures:

\[ z = \frac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - D_0}{SE} \]

Usually \(D_0 = 0\) when testing whether the two means are equal.

No: \( \sigma_1 \) and \( \sigma_2 \) are unknown

Use the sample standard deviations \(s_1\) and \(s_2\):

\[ SE = \sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}} \]

Use this for \(t\)-procedures:

\[ t = \frac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - D_0}{SE} \]

Usually \(D_0 = 0\) when testing whether the two means are equal.

Two Sample Proportions

Are you comparing two sample proportions \( \hat{p}_1 \) and \( \hat{p}_2 \)?

Confidence interval

Use the two observed sample proportions:

\[ SE = \sqrt{ \frac{\hat{p}_1(1-\hat{p}_1)}{n_1} + \frac{\hat{p}_2(1-\hat{p}_2)}{n_2} } \]

Confidence interval:

\[ (\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2) \pm \text{critical value}\cdot SE \]

Hypothesis test with \(H_0: p_1 = p_2\)

Use the pooled proportion:

\[ \hat{p}_{pool} = \frac{x_1 + x_2}{n_1 + n_2} \]

Then use:

\[ SE = \sqrt{ \hat{p}_{pool}(1-\hat{p}_{pool}) \left( \frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2} \right) } \]

Test statistic:

\[ z = \frac{(\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2) - 0}{SE} \]
Mean? Use \( \sigma/\sqrt{n} \) or \( s/\sqrt{n} \). Proportion? Use \( \sqrt{p(1-p)/n} \). Two samples? Add the two variances inside the square root.
